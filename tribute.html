Hedy Lamarr

Actress and Inventor

Early Life and Career

Hedy Lamarr was born in Vienna, Austria, and started her film career with "Ecstasy" in 1933. To escape her Nazi husband, she disguised herself as a maid and fled to the United States. In Hollywood, she signed with Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer and starred in the movie "Boom Town" with Clark Gable in 1940.

Contributions to Technology

In 1942, Hedy Lamarr and George Antheil co-invented a technology called frequency hopping spread spectrum. This technology, originally created for secure communication during World War II, became the basis for modern wireless technologies like WiFi and Bluetooth.

By Bevan Clark

Timeline of Hedy Lamarr's Life:

  • 1914: Hedy Lamarr (Hedwig Kiesler) is born on November 9 in Vienna, Austria.
  • 1933: She makes her film debut in the German movie "Ecstasy."
  • 1938: Hedy Lamarr moves to the United States and signs a contract with Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer (MGM).
  • 1940: Lamarr stars alongside Clark Gable in the film "Boom Town."
  • 1942: She co-invents frequency hopping spread spectrum technology with George Antheil.
  • 1960: Lamarr gets her star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame.
  • 1997: Hedy Lamarr and George Antheil receive the Electronic Frontier Foundation Pioneer Award, she is also the first woman to receive the Invention Convention's Bulbie Gnass Spirit of Achievement Award.
  • 2000: Hedy Lamarr passes away on January 19 in Casselberry, Florida.
  • 2014: Lamarr is inducted into the National Inventors Hall of Fame.

Hedy Lamarr was not only a talented actress, once known as the world's most beautiful woman. She also made significant contributions to technology, particularly in the field of wireless communications.
