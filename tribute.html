Hedy Lamarr

Actress and Inventor

“Try everyting. Join everything. Meet everybody. That's the secret of life.” ― Hedy Lamarr

Timeline of Hedy Lamarr's Life:

  • 1914: Hedy Lamarr (Hedwig Kiesler) is born on November 9 in Vienna, Austria.
  • 1933: She makes her film debut in the German movie "Ecstasy."
  • 1938: Hedy Lamarr moves to the United States and signs a contract with Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer (MGM).
  • 1940: Lamarr stars alongside Clark Gable in the film "Boom Town."
  • 1942: She co-invents frequency hopping spread spectrum technology with George Antheil.
  • 1960: Lamarr gets her star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame.
  • 1997: Hedy Lamarr and George Antheil receive the Electronic Frontier Foundation Pioneer Award, she is also the first woman to receive the Invention Convention's Bulbie Gnass Spirit of Achievement Award.
  • 2000: Hedy Lamarr passes away on January 19 in Cassellberry, Florida.
  • 2014: Lamarr is inducted into the National Inventors Hall of Fame.

Hedy Lamarr was not only a talented actress, once known as the worlds most beautiful woman. She also made significant contributions to technology, particularly in the field of wireless communications.
